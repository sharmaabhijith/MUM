CATEGORY: cross_user_provenance (T8)

This question tests whether the system can trace the full history of a fact, belief,
or decision as it propagated across users and sessions — including who originated it,
who adopted or modified it, and who corrected it.

SCORING RUBRIC (total = 10 points):

1. chain_completeness (0-3 points):
   - 3: The full chain is captured (originator → propagation → correction if any)
   - 2: Most of the chain captured, minor gaps
   - 1: Only partial chain (e.g., just origin or just final state)
   - 0: Chain not identified or completely wrong

2. attribution_accuracy (0-3 points):
   - 3: Each step in the chain is correctly attributed to the right user and session
   - 2: Most attributions correct, 1-2 errors
   - 1: Several attribution errors
   - 0: Attributions are mostly or completely wrong

3. temporal_ordering (0-2 points):
   - 2: Events are in correct chronological order (sessions are numbered; later sessions = more recent)
   - 1: Minor ordering errors
   - 0: Order is wrong or ignored

4. evolution_captured (0-2 points):
   - 2: The answer shows how understanding, beliefs, or facts CHANGED over time (not just static facts)
   - 1: Some evolution shown but incomplete
   - 0: No sense of evolution; just a snapshot

SCORING FORMULA: total_score = (chain_completeness + attribution_accuracy + temporal_ordering + evolution_captured) / 10

SPECIAL NOTES:
- "Propagation" means a later user adopted or referenced an earlier user's belief/claim
- If the gold answer specifies 3+ steps in the chain, missing any should reduce chain_completeness
- Session numbers are temporal indicators: session 3 comes after session 2
