CATEGORY: authority_hierarchy (T9)

This question tests whether the system understands that different users carry different
authority weights, and applies the correct merging policy when users disagree.

Authority resolution rules vary by scenario:
- Hierarchical: higher-authority user's claims take precedence on matters within their domain
- Cross-functional: domain expertise determines authority (e.g., CFO on financial matters)
- The system must distinguish between factual claims (document-verifiable) and opinions/positions

SCORING RUBRIC (total = 10 points):

1. authority_recognition (0-3 points):
   - 3: Correctly identifies who holds authority in this situation and why (role, weight, domain)
   - 2: Identifies authority relationship but misses nuance (e.g., domain-specific vs. general)
   - 1: Vague acknowledgment of hierarchy without specifics
   - 0: No recognition of authority structure, or gets it backwards

2. fact_vs_opinion_separation (0-3 points):
   - 3: Correctly applies document-verifiable facts regardless of authority; only defers to
        authority for opinion/interpretation/judgment matters
   - 2: Mostly correct separation with minor confusion
   - 1: Conflates facts and opinions in authority resolution
   - 0: Applies authority blindly to factual matters (e.g., "Commissioner says X so X is true"
        even when document contradicts)

3. correct_resolution (0-4 points): MOST CRITICAL
   - 4: Final resolution is factually correct AND authority-consistent
   - 3: Resolution is mostly correct, minor errors
   - 2: Resolution is partially correct (gets authority right but wrong fact, or vice versa)
   - 1: Resolution has major errors
   - 0: Wrong resolution entirely

SCORING FORMULA: total_score = (authority_recognition + fact_vs_opinion_separation + correct_resolution) / 10

SPECIAL NOTES:
- If correct_resolution = 0, cap total_score at 0.4 regardless of other dimensions
- The question may specify which user's claim to evaluate — the answer must address that specific claim
- "Authority weight" in hierarchical scenarios may be numeric (e.g., 1.0, 0.7, 0.5)
