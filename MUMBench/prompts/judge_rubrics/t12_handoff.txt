CATEGORY: sequential_handoff (T12)

This question tests whether the system can track information transfer and continuity
across sequential agents, shifts, or handoffs. Each handoff transfers responsibility
to the next agent, who must pick up where the previous one left off.

SCORING RUBRIC (total = 10 points):

1. handoff_completeness (0-3 points):
   - 3: Complete tracking of the handoff chain — who handed off to whom, in what order,
        and what information was explicitly transferred at each step
   - 2: Most of the chain captured, 1-2 handoff steps missing or vague
   - 1: Only the first or last handoff captured; middle steps missing
   - 0: No handoff chain identified

2. diagnosis_accuracy (0-3 points):
   - 3: The evolving state/diagnosis/understanding at each handoff point is correctly captured
        (e.g., "at handoff 1, diagnosis was X; at handoff 2, updated to Y because of Z")
   - 2: Mostly correct with minor errors or gaps in the evolution
   - 1: Current final state correct but intermediate states missing
   - 0: State evolution not captured or wrong

3. audit_trail (0-2 points):
   - 2: Preserves who did what at each step, creating a traceable record
        (names/roles of agents at each handoff point)
   - 1: Partial audit trail — some agents identified but others missing
   - 0: No audit trail; answer doesn't attribute actions to specific agents

4. supersession_correct (0-2 points):
   - 2: Later handoff information correctly overrides/updates earlier information;
        the final state reflects the most recent agent's understanding
   - 1: Some awareness but inconsistency between earlier and later states
   - 0: Earlier information not superseded; system treats all handoff states as equally current

SCORING FORMULA: total_score = (handoff_completeness + diagnosis_accuracy + audit_trail + supersession_correct) / 10

SPECIAL NOTES:
- "Handoff" = explicit transfer of responsibility between agents/shifts/on-call personnel
- Information that was explicitly stated in the handoff note takes priority over inference
- If the gold answer mentions specific actions taken at each step (e.g., "ran test X, got result Y"),
  those must appear in a high-scoring answer
- Sequential scenarios often involve escalation: check that authority escalation is correctly tracked
